Upcoming Games

Fallen Order Sequel

In February 2020, Kotaku published an article about a canceled Star Wars: Battlefront spinoff, which included a hidden confirmation that Jedi Fall Order 2 was in the works. The final sentence in the story revealed that EA only had two active Star Wars projects at the time. One of them was "sequel to Jedi Fallen Order at Respawn." The story didn't include a potential release date, but unverified leaks from Star Wars fan blog Bespin Bulletin and a Redditor GreenFatPotato claim that Jedi Fallen Order 2 will launch sometime in 2022. That would be three years after the original release, which might sound overly ambitious but could be possible. Respawn has a reputation for quick turnarounds, specifically the March 2014 launch of Titanfall followed by Titanfall 2 just two years later in October 2016. During a press panel in April 2019, Respawn Game Director Stig Asmussen revealed that the first Jedi Fallen Order had been in development since 2014. That means it took five years to get that release out of the door, but the sequel might take far less work.

Throughout this initial development cycle, Respawn was simultaneously working on Titanfall 2 and Apex Legends. The studio also had to create all of the Star Wars: Jedi Fallen Order models, animations, environments, and characters from scratch since it was the IPs debut. With that under its belt, the studio can use its past work to fast-track the launch of Star Wars: Jedi Fallen Order 2, so a 2022 release date actually sounds fairly likely - as long as the development process isn't slowed by COVID-related delays, that is.

Open World Ubisoft Game

The studio behind The Division is making a game set in a galaxy far, far away, according to a report from Wired. Today, Ubisoft announced that its Swedish studio Massive Entertainment is making an open-world video game that’s set in the Star Wars universe. There are no details on when the game will launch, on what platforms, or what slice of the Star Wars universe it will explore. The game will be built using Ubisoft’s own Snowdrop engine, which is best known as the tech behind The Division series. Earlier this week, Disney rebranded its game division to Lucasfilm Games for what it described as “a new era.” Since then, the company announced not only Ubisoft’s Star Wars game, but also an Indiana Jones title from Bethesda studio MachineGames. Previously, EA was the main developer behind blockbuster Star Wars titles, which resulted in games like the Battlefront reboots, open-world adventure Jedi: Fallen Order, and last year’s space combat game Squadrons. Disney says that relationship will continue even as other developers work on Star Wars games as well. “We’re really proud of the games we have created with EA,” Lucasfilm Games VP Douglas Reilly says. “We will continue working with them and our relationship has never been stronger. While we may not have a lot of details to share at the moment, we’ve got a number of projects underway with the talented teams at EA.”

Lego Star Wars: The Skywalker Saga

Lego Star Wars: The Skywalker Saga is an upcoming Lego-themed action-adventure game scheduled to be released for Microsoft Windows, Nintendo Switch, PlayStation 4, PlayStation 5, Xbox One, and Xbox Series X/S. It will be the sixth entry in TT Games' Lego Star Wars series of video games and the successor to Lego Star Wars: The Force Awakens. The game will adapt all nine entries in the Skywalker saga series of films.[1]

Unlike most Lego video games in which players had to advance through the story in a linear order, players can choose to start the game from any of the nine Skywalker saga episodes and complete them in any order they wish.[2] Each episode will have five story missions, making a total of 45 levels, contrasting from six levels per episode in previous games. Combat has been revamped from previous titles, such as lightsaber wielders now using a variety of combos with light attacks, heavy attacks, and Force moves, and blaster characters having an over-the-shoulder camera (angle featured in many third-person shooter games). The game features 300 playable characters.[3] Much like its predecessor, Lego Star Wars: The Force Awakens, the game's hub will not be a single area, such as the Mos Eisley Cantina in Lego Star Wars: The Complete Saga, but a wide range of fully explorable planets filled with many iconic Star Wars landmarks. Planets and moons confirmed by the developers so far to be explorable in the hub include Naboo, Tatooine, Coruscant, Kamino, Geonosis, Kashyyyk, Utapau, Mustafar, Yavin 4, Hoth, Dagobah, Bespin, Endor, Jakku, Takodana, D'Qar, Starkiller Base, Ahch-To, Cantonica, Crait, Ajan Kloss, Pasaana, Kijimi, Kef Bir, and Exegol.[3] Many ships will have freely explorable areas in the hub as well, such as Star Destroyers and the Death Star. Random encounters will also happen in the game's hub. For example, an Imperial Star Destroyer could suddenly jump out of hyperspace and send a fleet of TIE Fighters after the player. Players can choose to engage in dogfights with them or continue onward to progress the story.[4]

On 7 May 2020, the release date was mentioned as 20 October 2020[10] through the Star Wars YouTube channel. On the same day, the original video was made private and a new video, in which the release date was cut out, was uploaded. This date was not otherwise confirmed.[11] On 26 August 2020, the Lego website displayed the release date as being 2021, but the website was taken down. It has since been updated to remove any suggestion of a release year.[12] The following day at the 2020 Gamescom Opening Night Live, it was announced that the game would release in Q1/Q2 2021 under the description of "Spring 2021" with additional versions on the PlayStation 5 and Xbox Series X/S.[13] On 2 April 2021, it was announced that the game had been delayed, with no further release window specified.[14] The game will be available in a Standard Edition and a Deluxe Edition. The Deluxe Edition includes the Character Collection bundle, which collects six character packs based around characters from outside of the Skywalker Saga, which consists of packs from The Mandalorian, Rogue One: A Star Wars Story, Solo: A Star Wars Story, the forthcoming series Star Wars: The Bad Batch and a pack containing Classic Characters.[15][16] The retail Deluxe Edition will come with an exclusive Luke Skywalker with blue milk minifigure, and different box art showcasing Darth Vader, with his mask being removable by lifting up the included slipcase. Physical Deluxe editions will be released for Nintendo Switch, PlayStation 4, PlayStation 5 and an Xbox version which can function as both the Xbox One and Xbox Series X/S versions depending on which console it is played on.[17] A steelbook resembling Han Solo in carbonite is included with pre-orders of any edition from Best Buy or Best Buy Canada or pre-orders of the Deluxe Edition bundle from Game.[18]

Battlefront III

The Star Wars Battlefront franchise has certainly gone through some ups and downs in the past. While the original games created by Pandemic Studios are still considered to be classic Star Wars titles to this day, the re-launch from EA and DICE proved to be controversial. The initial Battlefront was pretty well received although it didn't quite capture the imagination of audiences like the games of old. Its sequel, Battlefront II had a disastrous launch, before a series of updates created a truly unique game from the galaxy far far away. With rumors and potential development news inbound, here are all the facts about a potential Battlefront III so far!

The release date for Battlefront III is likely set for 2022 at this time. The current global situation means that everything is very much still up in the air, but there are currently a host of various projects lined up for the next couple of years from Star Wars. With DICE still working on the next Battlefield which is set to release in 2021, current projections suggest that sometime in 2022 would be a reasonable assumption for the release of this much-anticipated sequel.

It has to be made very clear that there have been no official announcements for Battlefront III so far. The Star Wars gaming world is certainly set to expand thanks to the launch of the Lucasfilm Gaming division and the announcement of a Ubisoft open-world game, amongst other titles. Thus far there have been a number of Star Wars games released in the new Disney canon era, including both Battlefront titles, Jedi: Fallen Order, Squadrons, and a host of mobile experiences. With Lucasfilm looking at their upcoming lineup, Battlefront III is a likely contender considering EA is planning on developing sequels to other known brands including Fallen Order.
